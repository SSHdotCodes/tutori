Meet Tutori, an agentic voice tutor that teaches by speaking and drawing live. Ask any question by text or microphone. Tutori plans a visual lesson, then streams narration and hand-drawn whiteboard operations together. Lessons default to about three minutes, and can continue for up to ten. The agent can draw diagrams, graphs, equations, timelines, and callouts, or erase and clear the board when it needs room for the next idea. Follow-up questions keep the same lesson context, board, and learner profile.

Behind Tutori, GPT-5.6 Luna, accessed through OpenRouter, powers every planning, teaching, coaching, research-synthesis, memory, and whiteboard-vision role. It decides what to teach and emits structured operations such as boxes, arrows, labels, curves, plots, and erasures. A deterministic layout engine then fits text, routes callouts away from words, checks collisions, and animates each stroke in the browser. Whisper Large V3 Turbo transcribes voice questions, while GPT Audio Mini narrates at a calm, slightly slower pace.

For OpenAI Build Week, I used Codex as the primary engineering environment. Codex helped migrate the original Hugging Face Space to its production server, diagnose stuck loading, silent audio, and laggy send and example controls, and refactor the streaming lesson pipeline. It also helped design collision-aware board guardrails, longer continuous lessons, speech validation, regression tests, deployment checks, and this open-source release. The key decision was to let GPT-5.6 handle pedagogy and visual reasoning while ordinary code guarantees geometry, timing, and recovery.
